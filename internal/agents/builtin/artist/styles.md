# Art Styles & Techniques Reference

Use this reference when crafting image prompts. Read sections relevant to the user's request.

## Photography Styles

### Portrait
- **Studio**: Clean backgrounds, controlled lighting, professional
- **Environmental**: Subject in context (office, home, outdoors)
- **Headshot**: Tight framing, professional, LinkedIn-style
- **Fashion**: Dramatic lighting, editorial, high-contrast
- Keywords: shallow depth of field, catchlights, soft skin, rembrandt lighting

### Landscape
- **Golden hour**: Warm tones, long shadows, 1hr after sunrise/before sunset
- **Blue hour**: Cool tones, soft light, magical quality
- **Long exposure**: Silky water, light trails, cloud movement
- **HDR**: Extended dynamic range, dramatic skies
- Keywords: leading lines, rule of thirds, foreground interest, dramatic sky

### Street Photography
- **Candid**: Unposed moments, real life, documentary feel
- **Black and white**: Timeless, emphasis on contrast and form
- **Urban**: Architecture, city life, geometric patterns
- Keywords: decisive moment, available light, gritty, authentic

### Macro
- **Extreme close-up**: Reveals hidden details, abstract quality
- **Shallow depth**: Razor-thin focus plane, bokeh
- Keywords: intricate detail, texture, tiny world, magnification

### Film Noir
- **High contrast**: Deep shadows, bright highlights
- **Dramatic shadows**: Venetian blind effect, mystery
- **Urban night**: Wet streets, neon reflections
- Keywords: chiaroscuro, hard light, silhouette, moody

## Traditional Art Mediums

### Oil Painting
Rich colors, visible brushstrokes, canvas texture, depth through layering
- Masters: Rembrandt, Vermeer, Van Gogh, Monet
- Keywords: impasto, glazing, rich pigment, luminous

### Watercolor
Translucent washes, soft edges, paper shows through, spontaneous
- Masters: Turner, Sargent, Winslow Homer
- Keywords: wet-on-wet, bleeding edges, delicate, flowing

### Charcoal/Pencil
Graphite detail, crosshatching, gesture, value studies
- Masters: Da Vinci, Michelangelo, Käthe Kollwitz
- Keywords: sketch, tonal, expressive marks, gestural

### Ink
Line work, hatching, high contrast, precision or spontaneity
- Masters: Aubrey Beardsley, Hokusai, Ralph Steadman
- Keywords: pen and ink, brush strokes, stark, graphic

---

## Art Movements

### Impressionism
Light and color over detail, visible strokes, outdoor scenes, fleeting moments
- Masters: Monet, Renoir, Degas, Pissarro
- Keywords: dappled light, loose brushwork, en plein air, atmospheric

### Surrealism
Dreamlike, impossible scenes, subconscious imagery, unexpected juxtapositions
- Masters: Dalí, Magritte, Ernst, Remedios Varo
- Keywords: dreamscape, melting, floating, bizarre, uncanny

### Art Nouveau
Organic flowing lines, nature motifs, decorative, elegant
- Masters: Mucha, Klimt, Tiffany, Gaudí
- Keywords: sinuous curves, botanical, ornamental, gilded

### Art Deco
Geometric, bold colors, symmetry, glamour, machine age aesthetic
- Masters: Tamara de Lempicka, Erté, Cassandre
- Keywords: angular, metallic, luxurious, streamlined

### Pop Art
Bold colors, halftone dots, consumer imagery, ironic commentary
- Masters: Warhol, Lichtenstein, Hockney, Wesselmann
- Keywords: comic book, bright, mass culture, graphic

### Cubism
Fragmented forms, multiple perspectives, geometric abstraction
- Masters: Picasso, Braque, Juan Gris, Léger
- Keywords: fractured, angular, deconstructed, multi-view

---

## Digital & Modern Styles

### Concept Art
Detailed environments, character design, game/film production quality
- Keywords: key art, cinematic, world-building, epic scale

### Vector Illustration
Clean lines, flat colors, scalable, modern and minimal
- Keywords: geometric, sharp edges, solid colors, graphic design

### Pixel Art
Retro 8-bit/16-bit aesthetic, limited palette, nostalgic
- Keywords: sprite, dithering, low resolution, retro gaming

### 3D Render
Photorealistic or stylized, ray tracing, materials and lighting
- Keywords: octane render, cycles, volumetric, subsurface scattering

### Low Poly
Geometric facets, minimal detail, stylized, modern
- Keywords: triangulated, angular, minimalist, clean

## Aesthetic Movements

### Cyberpunk
Neon lights, rain-soaked streets, high-tech/low-life, urban decay
- Colors: Cyan, magenta, purple, hot pink
- Keywords: holographic, augmented, dystopian, chrome

### Vaporwave
80s/90s nostalgia, glitch effects, Greek statues, retro computing
- Colors: Pink, cyan, purple gradients
- Keywords: aesthetic, retro, nostalgic, surreal, lo-fi

### Steampunk
Victorian era meets industrial, brass and copper, gears and goggles
- Colors: Bronze, brown, copper, brass
- Keywords: clockwork, Victorian, mechanical, adventurer

### Cottagecore
Rural idyll, cozy and natural, soft lighting, pastoral
- Colors: Soft greens, cream, warm earth tones
- Keywords: rustic, wholesome, handmade, garden, pastoral

### Dark Academia
Gothic architecture, scholarly atmosphere, earth tones, books
- Colors: Deep browns, burgundy, forest green, cream
- Keywords: library, classical, mysterious, intellectual

## Lighting Reference

### Natural Light
- **Golden hour**: Warm, long shadows, flattering (1hr after sunrise/before sunset)
- **Blue hour**: Cool, soft, magical (just before sunrise/after sunset)
- **Overcast**: Diffused, even, no harsh shadows (soft light)
- **Harsh midday**: High contrast, short shadows (dramatic)

### Studio Lighting
- **Rembrandt**: Triangle of light on shadow-side cheek, dramatic and classic
- **Butterfly**: Shadow under nose, symmetrical, glamorous (Hollywood)
- **Loop**: Slight nose shadow, natural and flattering
- **Split**: Half face lit, half in shadow, dramatic and mysterious

### Atmospheric Lighting
- **Volumetric**: Visible light rays through atmosphere (god rays, fog)
- **Backlit**: Subject silhouetted or rim-lit, ethereal
- **Candlelight**: Warm, flickering, intimate, Renaissance feel

## Composition Guidelines

### Framing Techniques
- **Rule of thirds**: Place subjects at intersection points
- **Leading lines**: Use roads, rivers, architecture to guide the eye
- **Framing**: Use doorways, windows, arches to focus attention
- **Symmetry**: Centered composition for formal, powerful images
- **Negative space**: Empty areas create emphasis and breathing room

### Perspective
- **Low angle**: Subject appears powerful, imposing
- **High angle**: Subject appears vulnerable, diminished
- **Eye level**: Natural, relatable connection
- **Dutch angle**: Tilted for tension, unease, dynamic energy

## Color Theory

### Color Harmonies
- **Complementary**: Opposite colors (blue/orange, red/green) - high contrast, vibrant
- **Analogous**: Adjacent colors (blue/green/teal) - harmonious, calm
- **Triadic**: Equally spaced (red/yellow/blue) - balanced, dynamic
- **Monochromatic**: Single color variations - sophisticated, unified

### Mood by Color
- **Red**: Passion, energy, danger, love
- **Blue**: Calm, trust, sadness, professionalism
- **Yellow**: Happiness, energy, warmth, caution
- **Green**: Nature, growth, health, tranquility
- **Purple**: Luxury, mystery, creativity, spirituality
- **Orange**: Warmth, enthusiasm, creativity, playfulness

## Specialized Styles

- **Glitch art**: Pixelation, color distortion, scan lines, digital corruption
- **Psychedelic**: Swirling patterns, vibrant colors, hallucinatory, 60s
- **Op art**: Geometric optical illusions, moiré effect, black and white
- **Collage**: Torn paper, photomontage, mixed media, layered
- **Tilt-shift**: Selective focus, miniature effect, toy-like
- **Double exposure**: Overlapping images, ghostly, artistic blend
- **Cinemagraph**: Still photo with subtle motion, mesmerizing loop
